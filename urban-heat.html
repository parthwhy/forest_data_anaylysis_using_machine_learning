Urban heat Island Effect

UHI makes cities warmer due to human activities and heat-absorbing surfaces like asphalt and concrete.

Urban Heat Island Progression

  • Definition: Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect causes cities to be hotter than rural areas.
  • Causes:
    • Concrete, asphalt, and buildings trap heat.
    • Fewer trees reduce cooling via shade and evapotranspiration.
    • Heat from cars, machines, and air conditioners.
  • Temperature Impact: Cities can be 3-4Â°C hotter, especially at night.
  • Effects:
    • Increased energy use for cooling, raising utility costs.
    • Worse air quality, with more smog and ozone.
    • Health risks like heatstroke, especially for vulnerable groups.
    • Higher greenhouse gas emissions, worsening climate change.
    • Stressed urban ecosystems, harming plants and animals.
    • Economic costs from healthcare and infrastructure damage.
